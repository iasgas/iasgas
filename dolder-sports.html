Great potential lies in using the Water that is stored in the swimming pool during the summer months. Instead of draining it into the canalisation at the end of the season to clean and maintain the basin, a more sensible cycle is created. In order to use the water safely, the current chlorine pool is reimagined as a naturally filtered pool.

The filtration tower which is part of the intervention, purifies the water and also bridges the topographical gap between the Pool and the neighbouring ice skating rink. Using a part of the water that was used in the pool throughout the summer, we can provide the the ice skating rink with water come the winter months.

As we move on we encounter the filtration tower. Water is pumped up into the tower and released into dry filters. After passing through this initial filter, it drips into the wet filter, from where the water is let back into the pool.

You can also choose to take a step inside the tower where you enter a quiet, tranquil room. Here you can sit and listen to the water dripping down into a small basin.

In fall, most of the water leaves the pool, supplying the ice rink as well as the forest with water. A small percentage of the water is left inside and heated by the excess heat that is generated by the cooling system the ice rink relies upon. This leaves a shallow basin to bathe in, during the colder months.

The newly planned elongated structure makes the different levels of the terrain accessible and forms a new entrance situation oriented towards the Dolderbahn and the city of Zurich. It acts as a connecting element for the entire site. Spaces are framed along the structure, as seen at the entrance or the bathing area. Both the new uses and the existing buildings are connected to the path on the two sides.

Seasonality defines the site. The prevailing uses require large amounts of water, and at the end of the season, the water is fed into the sewage system. The aim was to create cycles and to repurpose the water on site and minimise the need for new water to support the seasonal uses.

At the same time, a large part of the Pools water is let down into the forest where it ends up in seepage ponds that allow the water to infiltrate and passively irrigate the whole forest, creating new life.

Forests have been becoming increasingly dry, which increases the risk for wildfires. This forces the city to place restrictions on the use of these recreational areas. Another prevalent issue that comes with increased aridity is the spread of bark beetles. During dry spells, trees are not able to produce as much resin, leaving them vulnerable to the invasive species.
