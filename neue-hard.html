At the corner of the building assigned to me, heavily trafficked roads pass by on two sides. The resulting noise pollution is a problem for residential construction, which is why public uses were placed on the lower floors. This way, the building engages with pedestrians moving under the Hardbrücke. Through cross-financing, a mix of uses is created. Less profitable uses are subsidized by more profitable ones, such as a supermarket. These, in turn, benefit from the increased visitor traffic generated by uses such as the exhibition space.

Nestled between modern residential buildings and industrial structures, the massive office building of the Zürcher Kantonalbank is situated at the Hardbrücke. Since the 1970s, its distinctive architecture has shaped the image of the district. However, times are changing, and with the COVID-19 pandemic, the way we work has changed. Because of the growing popularity of home office, the Zürcher Kantonalbank is hard-pressed to use all the space available in the building. In collaboration with the bank, the semester's task was to imagine ways to fit housing into the existing structure.

Uta Graff's book “black spaces” was an exciting starting point for the development of the floor plans. Due to the building depth of the existing structure, low-light or lightless rooms inside the building are unavoidable. These “unattractive,” or nearly unusable rooms for residential purposes, can provide space for uses such as cinemas or climbing gyms. Such leisure activities can quickly be pushed out of city centers because otherwise land prices would be unaffordable. The black spaces can thus enable leisure activities close to residential areas.

On the roof, a wooden extension is constructed to provide space for a hydroponic system. This form of cultivation allows for the production of fresh food in an urban environment without relying on traditional agricultural land. A local association from the building is in charge of the management, promoting not only self-sufficiency but also fostering a stronger community.
